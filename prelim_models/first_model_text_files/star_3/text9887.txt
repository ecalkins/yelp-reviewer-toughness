0	It was pretty good. I got the Tonkotsu shoyu ramen. Portions were not as big as some of the other ramen places in Toronto. The options of noodle choices were confusing - original, thin, and thick. The original and the thin noodles taste exactly the same. I would definitely try the thick noodles next time (if there is one). The broth is good but not as flavorful as Kinton's. The ambiance at Kinton is also better since you get to watch them cook the noodles.
